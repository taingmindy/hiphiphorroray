Horror Subgenres

Hover over each image to learn about the different horror subgenres, and click the red link to explore the movies within them

Psychological

This sub-genre focuses on mental, psychological, and emotional states that the characters go through, using them as a way to relate and scare the audience simultaneously.

Zombie

Zombies are fictional creatures usually portrayed as reanimated corpses or virally infected human beings.

Paranormal

Paranormal horror focuses on the supernatural, such as spirits, demons, possession, and phenomena beyond the scope of normal scientific understanding.

Slasher

Slasher films involve a killer, usually masked, stalking and murdering groups of people.

Comedic

A type of horror film in which the macabre are portrayed using a humorous, tongue-in-cheek approach.

Monster

This subgenre features monsters from science fiction and dark fantasy, such as aliens, vampires, werewolves, and more.

Folk

Folk horror uses elements of folklore to invoke fear--typical elements include a rural setting and themes of isolation, religion, nature, and the potential darkness of rural landscapes.
